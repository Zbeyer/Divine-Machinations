Down Time Points (DTP) - Roll against encounter tables.

1 DTP is 1 hour of down time.

Downtime activities are instantly accomplished but the DM may wait until the next session to resolve them if the logistics are time consuming: keep the game rolling, down time activities are lower priority. Examples include:

  • Carousing or Relaxation - Default activity. Spend time between sessions relaxing
  • Crafting - make non-magical items (herbalism, smithing, alchemy, weaving, etc.)
  • Construction - Build buildings like houses, shops, and airships (this will often cost in materials or gp, permits or gp, and land or gp)
  • Research & Investigation - Learn more about a subject or substance or uncover secrets related to something from a prior session
  • Practicing a Profession - Work a job or serve at a temple or manage a buisness
  • Training - Aquire new skills, ability scores, or features from a teacher

Experianc XP - Is a magic currency to unlock upgrades and infuse them into items called power stones.

Player Characters (PCs) can only unlock features and spells at or below a class level they have unlocked.

Player Characters (PCs) can then infuse unlocked fatures and spells into the power stones of their gear.

NPCs can buy and sell XP as well as PCs earn it.

Power stones have a limit to how many spells, keywords, boons, features, etc. that can be infused into them.

Features, keywords, enhancemets (+1 modifiers), etc. infused into a power stone is called an infusion.

  • You may only infuse things you have unlocked.

    • No additional hit dice are given when leveling. Instead a feature can be infused into a power stone to increase max health of the user (see rule below).

    Money - trade between NPCs.

    1. CP - copper pieces (1 dram)
    2. SP - silver pieces (1 dram)
    3. GP - gold pieces (1 dram)
      4.
    4. PP - platinum pieces (1 dram)

    Prices are often measured in GP but other precious metals are accepted. Silver is 1/10 the valu of gold and copper 1/10 the value of silver. Platinum is 10x the value of gold. So like vanilla DnD. 10:1 carries across the main coins CP | SP | GP | PP.

    Banks give different interest rates depending on what you do. A normal account might have a 2% interest rate per year but a CD might be 6%. Managing money is important. DM's can roll or decide if investments were good or bad.

    Loans also have differrent interest rates, and thieves guild loans are even more risk / reward.

    Selling magic items is usualally the value of the powerstone +25 GP for each infuion.

    Buying magic items is much more expensive. It is common to see prices 100x the sell value. Haggling merchants down from listed price is hard but not impossible.

    Coin rolls and written statements are commonly used by banks and merchants that transfer a large volume of Valuables. It is safe to assume trades of more than 500 GP in value starts with a contract.

    Service Robots

    Commoners often have constructs or robots follow them with power stones to do things like prestidigitation, magic missile, or extra dimensional storage.

    These bots whether they fly or walk are often used as inventory storage and a coin purse as well as protection. Robots thend to continue fighting long after their owner dies.

    Robots can be installed with custom firearms. It is not impossible for a robot to have a minigun that shoots 10 times per attack and/or a rocket launcher that uses a large projectile on par with a fireball.

    Robots can be aquired from artisians and are considered pets regardless of intelligence. A warforged is legally a pet. Sentient machines avoid cities for this reason.

    Robots often have powerstones with spells and spell slots infused.

    Robots consume on average 1 leter of Sparkle Dew a month.

    There is no limit to how many pets you may have but more than three may raise eybrows.

    Sparkle Refinery

    Fuel refineries make Sparkle Dew consumed by robots, magitech, vehicles, and pretty much all machines. Sparkle Dew is highly flamable. Probably even explosive.

    Glimmer Refinery

    Glimer refineries make glimmer water wich is used to keep magitec running. Glimmer water is effectively liquid magic.

    Magic items consume it in very small doses but since the average person has 3 or more magic items tons are shipped out. The average person consumes one fluid ounce every six months.

    Workers avoid speaking in common or elvish around it. many work in silence. Refinery wo don't want to accidentally invoke a spell.

    Alchemy Instructions

    Use natiure to find and harvest plants

    Color determines potion tyoe, Example red is a health potion.

    1. Get Lumin Essence
    2. Infuse a plant in the essance like tea (takes 12h) to get a T1 potion.
    3. Use the previous potion to infuse another plant to get a stronger potion.
    4. Keep using lower potions and plants until the potion is the Tier you want.

    There are many colors of plants. Red is for HP, Blue for spell slots.
